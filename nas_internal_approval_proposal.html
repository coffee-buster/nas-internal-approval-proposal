Small Office NAS, Network, UPS, and Printer Proposal

Internal approval proposal for a Toronto-based office of approximately 10 staff. The recommendation prioritizes reliable shared file storage, Google Drive backup/sync, graceful UPS shutdown, simple wired expansion, and a separate colour laser multifunction printer budget.

Prepared: May 12, 2026 Prices: CAD, pre-tax NAS/network budget target: under $1,500 Printer: separate budget

Executive Summary

The recommended core setup is a Synology DS225+ with two 4TB WD Red Plus NAS drives in RAID1, a USB-capable UPS, and an unmanaged TP-Link gigabit switch. This provides a simple, supportable platform for shared company files, Google Drive synchronization/backup, and future light automation or Docker use.

Recommended approval: Proceed with the balanced NAS/network package estimated at $1,247.95 pre-tax, excluding printer and any cabling/labour.

Implementation Notes

  • Configure NAS with RAID1 / SHR one-drive redundancy using two matched 4TB NAS-rated drives.
  • Create shared folders by business function and apply user/group permissions.
  • Enable Synology Drive or Cloud Sync for Google Drive workflows after confirming desired direction: backup, two-way sync, or one-way archive.
  • Connect UPS to the NAS via USB and configure DSM safe shutdown on low battery.
  • Keep modem/router, switch, and NAS on battery-backed UPS outlets.
  • Schedule update windows and alert notifications for disk health, backup status, and UPS events.

Risks and Considerations

  • RAID1 protects against one drive failure but does not protect against accidental deletion, ransomware, theft, fire, or NAS failure.
  • A separate offsite/cloud backup policy should be approved after NAS deployment.
  • Retailer pricing and availability can change quickly; final procurement should re-check live pages or request a business quote.
  • UPS compatibility should be verified against Synology DSM compatibility or confirmed by vendor before purchase.
  • Third-party printer toner may reduce cost but can increase quality, reliability, and warranty-support risk; OEM supplies are recommended for business use.
